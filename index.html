Research & Innovation

On-board Power Electronic DC-DC Converters

Developing compact, cost-effective, and efficient DC-DC converters for electric vehicles, including onboard chargers (OBC) and auxiliary power modules (APM). The core focus of my Ph.D. thesis at IIT Bombay.

Integrated DC-DC Stage

  • Proposed a low-cost three-port integrated power converter combining OBC and APM stages with minimal device count.
  • Designed and simulated port-decoupling controllers using PLECS.
  • Fabricated a hardware prototype integrating a 1.5 kW OBC and 350 W APM for full practical validation.

Design Methodologies

Proposed three distinct IPC design approaches and identified the most suitable for two-wheeler EV applications, benchmarking against market alternatives.

Light-Load Efficiency Improvement of SDABC

  • Identified significant efficiency loss in the semi-dual active bridge converter (SDABC) under light-load conditions.
  • Proposed design-based strategies to improve overall energy efficiency.

Soft-Switching Range Extension

Analysed ZVS loss in SDABC under varying load conditions and developed a design-centric approach to extend soft-switching across the charging profile.
